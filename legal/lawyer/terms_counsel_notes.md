# Annex A: Notes for New Zealand Counsel (Terms of Use)

> This annex is for the reviewing lawyer only and is not part of the customer-facing document. It was prepared by the operator (not a lawyer) to flag points that need professional review. The whole document requires review by a qualified New Zealand lawyer before publication beyond a closed beta.

**General.** The operator is currently a sole individual with no registered entity, located in New Zealand. Registering a NZ limited liability company before a public launch is the single largest liability mitigation and is planned. Users are Israelis worldwide, creating multi-jurisdictional exposure (NZ + GDPR for EU users). There are no real users yet beyond the operator. The binding version is English; a Hebrew translation is provided for users, with a clause stating English prevails subject to mandatory local consumer law.

**Section 1 (Acceptance / Eligibility).** Minimum age 16, consistent with the Privacy Policy. Confirm the eligibility and competence wording.

**Section 2 (Availability, as is / as available).** Confirm the disclaimer against the limits of the NZ Consumer Guarantees Act (see Section 9).

**Section 4 (User content / license).** This license was balanced to resolve the tension with the deletion/erasure right. Confirm the license scope and the revocability wording of the "promotional use" sub-clause.

**Section 5 (Prohibited content / moderation).** Added categories beyond the original list: illegal content, and spam or non-recipe content (ties to moderation strikes).

**Section 9 (Warranty disclaimer / liability cap / food safety).** Highest priority. Confirm the NZD 200 aggregate liability cap, the food-safety and allergen disclaimer, and the interaction with the Consumer Guarantees Act 1993 and the Fair Trading Act 1986. The cap and exclusions may be limited or unenforceable against consumers.

**Section 11 (Suspension and blocking).** Aligned with the in-app "Blocked" screen (an appeal is offered, resolving an earlier internal contradiction). Confirm the record-keeping/evidence-retention wording. Note a separate, mandatory CSAM path: suspected child sexual abuse material must not be stored, and must be reported to the NZ Department of Internal Affairs. This requires a dedicated procedure and counsel input before any evidence store is operated.

**Section 12 (Termination / account deletion).** Confirm the survival clause and its interaction with the deletion right in the Privacy Policy.

**Section 15 (Governing law / jurisdiction).** NZ governing law and courts, with recognition of consumers' mandatory local rights. Confirm the enforceability of the forum-selection clause against global consumers.

**Section 16 (General / language / assignment).** Confirm the language clause (English binding, Hebrew translation) and the assignment provision (transfer of the business to a future entity).

**Contact addresses.** The copyright@ and support@ mailboxes referenced in the document do not yet exist and must be established before launch.
